Team Name: Dynamo
Bot Strategy Description: The High-Performance Alpha-Beta Minimax Engine

Our competitive Chain Reaction agent utilizes a deeply optimized Minimax architecture with Alpha-Beta pruning, specifically engineered for the 12x8 board dimensions under strict 1.0s computational limits.

1. 🔍 High-Depth Alpha-Beta Search
Instead of the stochastic nature of MCTS, our bot uses deterministic Iterative-Deepening Minimax. By focusing the search budget on the most strategically viable moves (Top-15 Candidate Pruning), the bot explores 6–10 turns into the future. Alpha-Beta pruning aggressively eliminates millions of irrelevant board states, focusing pure CPU power on lines that maximize leverage.

2. 🗄️ Transposition Table & Dihedral Symmetry
To maximize efficiency in a game defined by deterministic cascades, we implement a global Transposition Table with Zobrist Hashing. 
- Dihedral Symmetry Mapping: Every board state is mapped to its 4 geometric rotations and reflections before hashing. This canonicalization quadruples the effective size of our search cache, allowing the bot to "remember" evaluations from previous turns and skip redundant calculations across all 4 quadrants of the board.

3. 🛰️ Chebyshev Positional Heatmap
The bot's evaluation function is driven by a smooth Chebyshev Heatmap. Unlike simple corner-weighting, this distance-decay model (Corners=1.0, Edges=0.8, Interior=0.2) gives the bot a granular understanding of board leverage. It treats the board as a high-pressure manifold, organically prioritizing territory expansion that "smothers" the opponent's movement.

4. 🛡️ Optimized "Unkillable" Physics
The cascade simulation (explosion logic) is natively optimized in Python to prevent infinite loops. By tracking piece counts (`p0_count`, `p1_count`) directly inside the cascade queue, the bot recognizes winning/losing terminal states mid-explosion. If the opponent is eliminated during a chain reaction, the simulation breaks immediately, completing the move and securing the win without wasting computational cycles.

5. ⏱️ 0.75s Hardware-Safe Deadline
To ensure strict compliance with the 1.0s timeout and protect against OS-level CPU spikes, the bot monitors its execution budget via a high-precision timer. At exactly 0.75 seconds, the search is gracefully aborted, and the bot returns the best move discovered at its deepest fully-searched ply, leaving a 25% safety margin for OS overhead.
